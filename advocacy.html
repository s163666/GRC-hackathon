#5: Over two-thirds of low-income women do not struggle to cover the cost of menstrual products. (F)

According to an article by Reuters, two-thirds [of surveyed low-income women in the US] couldn't afford menstrual hygiene products such as tampons or pads during the previous year.

#8: In the U.S., menstrual products are taxed in only 14 states. (F)

15 states have exempted period products from sales tax, while 35 states in the US have taxes on menstrual products.

#9: Period poverty is linked to symptoms of moderate-to-severe depression. (T)

68.1% of college-attending women participants who experienced period poverty had symptoms of moderate-to-severe depression.

#10: Nearly nine in ten people who menstruate agree that period poverty is a public health issue. (F)

The majority of people who menstruate (68%) recognize that period poverty is a public health issue in the United States.
